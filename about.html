Royal Enfield is a motorcycle company that manufactures classic and off-road motorcycles. Here are some things to know about Royal Enfield:

History

The first Royal Enfield motorcycle was built in 1901 by The Enfield Cycle Company in Redditch, England. The company's motto is "Made Like a Gun", which describes their motorcycles as simple, resilient, and dependable.

Production

Royal Enfield is the oldest global motorcycle brand in continuous production. The Royal Enfield Bullet is the longest-lived motorcycle design in history, having remained in production since 1932.

Products

Royal Enfield makes a variety of motorcycles, including the Bullet, Classic 350, Thunderbird, Meteor 350, Classic 500, Interceptor 650, Continental, and Hunter 350.

Military use

During World War II, Royal Enfield produced large quantities of military motorcycles, bicycles, generators, and ant-aircraft gun predictors. The 125cc "Airborne" motorcycle, also known as the Flying Flea, was a popular model.

Ownership

In 1994, Eicher Motors, an Indian multinational automobile manufacturing company, took over ownership of Royal Enfield. The company is now headquartered in Chennai, India.
